## Chap 1 - Production For Subsistence
- This chapter covers commodity production and usage of a simple economies with few goods that do not produce any surplus. 
- All commodities are used in the production of other commodities, ex
  280 qr. wheat + 12 t. iron $\to$ 400 qr. wheat
  120 qr. wheat + 8t. iron $\to$ 20 t. iron
	- Sraffa labels the exchange values of these two commodities to preserve distribution of commodities as 10 qr. of wheat for 1t. of iron. 
### Generalization of Subsistence Commodity Economy
Let 'a', 'b', ..., 'k' be commodities, each produced by a separate industry
Let 'A', 'B', ..., 'K' be the quantity produced of 'a', 'b', etc respectively.
Let $A_a, B_a, ...$ be the required quantities of each commodity required in the production of 'a'. 
- All of these represent known quantities. The unknowns t.b.d are $p_a, p_b, ..., p_k$ respectively the values of 'a', 'b', ..., 'k', which if adopted will restore the initial position. 
**The Conditions of Production are as Follows:**
$$A_a p_a + B_a p_b + ... + K_a p_k = A p_a $$
$$A_b p_a + B_b p_b + ... + K_b p_k = B p_b $$
$$A_k p_a + B_k p_b + ... + K_k p_k = K p_k $$
Where the sum of the first column is equal to the first line, the sum of the second column is equal to the second line, etc. by nature of this being a subsistence system. It should be noted that any of the values  on the LHS can be 0. 
One commodity is to be taken as the standard of value and all others are pegged to this standard, leaving k-1 unknowns, which is solvable as there are k-1 independent linear equations to determine the prices.
