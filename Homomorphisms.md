## Def 1: Homomorphism
- Let G and H be [groups](Groups). 
  A function $f: G \to H$ is called a **group homomorphism** if $f(xy) = f(x) f(y), \; \forall x,y \in G$  
- Note particularly that the product xy on the left uses the multiplication in G, where the product on the right uses the product in H[]().
