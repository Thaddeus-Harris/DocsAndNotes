## Def
Let $G$ be a finite abelian [[Group]] and $\mathbb{F}$ a field, then $L^2(G,\mathbb{F})$ is the set of functions $f: G \to F$.  We imbue it with the structure of a vector space by pointwise addition and scaling. i.e: $$(\alpha f)(x) = \alpha f(x)$$
and $$(f+g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)$$
## Prop (Orthogonality)
- The [[character]]s $\hat G \subset L^2(G)$ form an orthogonal basis. $$<e_{1} e_{2} > = \begin{cases}  
0 \;\text{if} \;e_1 \neq e_2 \\
|G| \;\text{if} \;e_1 = e_2 
\end{cases}  $$
